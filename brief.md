# Online Retail Sales Analysis: Business Brief

## What the Data Shows
The United Kingdom is the dominant revenue market, which is expected 
given it is the business's home base. Revenue peaks in November at 
approximately 3 million, with December a close second, confirming that 
sales are heavily driven by the festive and end of year period. The most 
demanded product by total revenue is the Regency Cakestand 3 Tier. 
However when November is isolated, the top selling product switches to 
the Paper Chain Kit, suggesting festive decoration and craft products 
drive the peak month rather than the business's regular top performers.

## Recommendations
1. Ensure all inventory is fully stocked ahead of October to avoid 
   missing the festive influx, demand is highest in the final three 
   months of the year.

2. Stock festive decoration and craft products heavily ahead of November 
   as data shows these categories, not the overall top sellers, drive 
   the peak month revenue.

3. February is the lowest grossing month. A targeted Valentine's Day 
   campaign focused on gift products could unlock an underutilised 
   revenue window.

4. The UK currently generates the majority of revenue, which creates 
   concentration risk if domestic demand drops. Further investigation 
   into underperforming international markets is recommended to build 
   a more diversified revenue base.

## What to Monitor
- Track inventory levels monthly, particularly for the top 10 products
- Flag any item that carries high restocking costs but consistently 
  underperforms on revenue and reconsider its place in the product mix
- Ensure top performing products never run low during the October to 
  December peak period
- Monitor whether average order value is growing or shrinking month 
  by month
